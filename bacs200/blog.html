Climate Change

October 16, 2020 by Edgar Diaz

Climate Change is one of the only things that effects everyone. This issue has no biase and will only continue to worsen if no greater action is taken soon.

So, what exactly is it? Climate Change can be described as the change in weather phenomena within a region over a long period of time. Climate is different from place to place and offers a variety of conditions depending on typography and seasons.

Earth’s Climate is derived by combining all the climates around the world together.So, when referencing climate change, we are referencing the irregularities within regions. These irregularities might seem to be insignificant alone but when added together, they can be disastrous for our planet. Nasa scientist have been studying the global climate for years and findings have confirmed that the earths overall temperature has risen by one-degree Fahrenheit, just within the last 100 years. This one degree might once again seem insignificant but the changes that we are experiencing now are anything but that.

The fact of the matter is that the warming of the Earth’s climate has caused massive snow and ice to melt within the artic. The warming has caused sea levels to rise and has even changed the timing of when certain plants grow.

Since, every region has different climate some regions will get hotter and some will get much colder. Some places will get longer winters with more snow and vise versa. Other regions will see more rain, and some will encounter never-ending drought. The affects of climate change are truly devasting and must not be taken lightly.

Understanding the Argument

What is the argument against climate change?

One of the greatest arguments against climate change is that human beings are to small and insignificant to change temperature levels on a global scale. Science on the other hand tell us something different. For the world population has reached 7.8 Billion people. We might seem insignificant when standing alone but once we add everything up, we can see that humans have a greater role to play than we originally thought.

Our consumption and waste have given us empirical evidence that humans must come to new collective consciousness when thinking about climate change.

Social implications

So how can you help the fight against climate change?

For starters you can change the way you consume water and energy. Changing your habits to consume less and waste less resources is crucial in fighting climate change. Simply changing these habits will aid in lowering carbon pollution.

One can also invest in energy-efficient appliances or better home efficient light bulbs and if applicable you can change the way you commute to work by car pooling or riding a bike if possible.

All these changes are relatively small and will aid in lowering one’s carbon footprint. Anyone person can make a difference if they chose to do so.

Economic implications

What are some economic implications caused by Climate Change?

Well for starters if you’re a fan of traveling climate change will indefinitely affect tourism and recreation activities. A warming climate and changes in precipitation patterns will decrease the number of days when recreational winter activities such as skiing, and snowmobiling can take place. In the Southwest and Mountain West, we will see an increase in the number of wildfires. This in turn will affect hiking and recreation in parks. Furthermore, beaches could suffer erosion due to sea level rise and storm surge. Also, changes in the migration patterns of fish and animals would affect fishing and hunting.

Climate change might also make it harder and more expensive for people to insure their homes, businesses, or other valuable assets in risk-prone areas, or preclude them from insurance altogether and this is only the beginning.

Political implications

What are some political implications of climate change?

Political implications will be seen through our elected officials for they will be the ones who will have the last say when protecting our environment.

Right now, depending on where you reside, chances are that there are some sort of regulations and provisions in place to protect and preserve your area. However, that is not the same for everyone. For even in places such as the US, there are millions of Americans who don’t have access to clean water.

Electing proper Politicians is crucial, for if uncaring officials are placed into power they will be the ones to blame for:

  • Poor drinking water.
  • Greater air pollution
  • The contamination of soil and water by toxic waste.
  • Damage to Earth’s ozone layer
  • Acid rain

For use to avoid these catastrophes we must be vigilant and elect leaders that know and understand the science of Climate Change.

Conclusion

Ultimately, we are not so far off the deepened that we cannot reverse our course of action. However, this is not a fight that can be won single handedly. Each and everyone of us has to do their part in this battle. We each hold a piece of the puzzle that will ultimately decide the fate our children and of our planet. The fate of the world is literally within our hands and we will either be lauded by future generations or vilified by them. This is our only home and we must do whatever we can to protect it for us and future generations.
